FAQs

Common questions

What file formats do you accept?
We accept AI (Adobe Illustrator), DWG (AutoCAD), EPS, PDF and ZIP archives. All files must be at 1:1 scale in millimetres. Vector format is required for 2D cutting. If your files are in a different format, contact us before submitting — we can often advise on conversion.
How long does CNC machining take?
Turnaround depends on the complexity and volume of the job. Most standard jobs are completed within a few days. We'll confirm an exact lead time when we quote. If you have a tight deadline, tell us upfront and we'll do our best to accommodate it.
Is there a minimum order?
No minimum order. We cut single prototype parts as readily as production runs of hundreds. Pricing is based on machine time and material — not order volume.
What materials can you cut?
We regularly cut MDF, plywood, hardwood, aluminium, acrylic, high-density foam, Dibond, Forex, HDPE, Nylon, Delrin and PETG. We cut up to 70mm depth in a single pass. For thicker work, we can develop a cutting and layering plan.
What is the maximum cutting depth?
Our Pegasus 1530 ATC cuts to 70mm depth in a single pass. For projects requiring greater material thickness, we can work with you on a layered construction approach — feasibility depends on the material and design.
Where are you based?
We're based in Walthamstow, East London — 22 Rigg Approach, E10 7QN, operated by Rule One Productions. Finished parts can be collected from the workshop or sent by courier.
What machine do you use?
We run a Pegasus 1530 ATC CNC router — a professional-grade machine with a 1500×3000mm bed, 9kW Italian HSD spindle, Japanese Yaskawa servo motors and a 12-tool automatic tool changer.
CNC routing vs laser cutting — what's the difference?
CNC routing uses a spinning cutter and excels with thicker materials, structural parts and 3D relief work. Laser cutting suits very thin, delicate sheet work. We offer CNC routing and milling — contact us if you're unsure which process suits your job.
